Sales Workflow

Sales cycle, lead management, visit process, account tiers, and follow-up standards.

Sales Cycle — 8 Stages
Stage 1
Prospecting Client
Search for potential B2B buyers — pharmacies, clinics, wholesale distributors. Use lead directory, LinkedIn, industry events, referrals. Segment into Hot / Warm / Cold.
Stage 2
Make Initial Contact
First outreach by phone, email, or walk-in. Goal: book a meeting only — do not pitch product on first contact. Pharmacy: ask for Dispensary Manager. Clinic: go through Practice Manager.
Stage 3
Product Presentation
In-person or video meeting. Lead with their pain point (stockouts, inconsistent COA, slow delivery). Match SKU to indication. Show COA + terpene matrix. Max 3 SKUs per first meeting.
Stage 4
Overcome Objection
Listen actively. Never argue. Acknowledge the objection, bridge to Thai Stick solution. Use master objection sheet. Common objections: 'we have a supplier', 'grown in Thailand', 'not TGA registered'.
Stage 5
Close the Sale
Ask for a trial order or commitment. For pharmacies: trial order at reduced rate. For doctors: ask them to prescribe for 2–3 patients over 30 days. For wholesale: propose 90-day pilot.
Stage 6
Nurture Prospects
For leads not yet ready: maintain contact every 2–4 weeks. Share new batch COAs, clinical updates, market news. Keep Thai Stick top of mind without pressure.
Stage 7
Qualifying Leads
Before spending time on a lead, confirm: Are they licensed? Do they have S8 storage? Do they have prescribing/dispensing authority? What is their monthly volume? Budget?
Stage 8
Referrals & Repeat Sales
After 2nd–3rd successful order, ask: "Is there another pharmacy or clinic in your network we should meet?" Referrals are the highest-converting lead source in Australian medical cannabis.

Dispensary / Clinic Visit — Step by Step
Step 1 — Prepare
Research the Account
Research patient volume, current supplier, main indications. Confirm appointment with decision-maker. Bring: product sheet, COA summary, terpene matrix, business card, notebook.
Step 2 — Arrive
Introduce Yourself
Ask for the Dispensary Manager or Practice Manager by name. "Hi, I'm [Name] from Thai Stick — we're a GMP-certified cannabis supplier from Perth. I have a 20-minute appointment."
Step 3 — Open
Lead with Their Pain Point
Before showing any product: "Before I walk you through our range — could I ask, what does your current cannabis supply arrangement look like? Specifically around reliability and lead times?" Listen fully. Take notes.
Step 4 — Present
Thai Stick Supply Chain
In 5 sentences: Thailand Cultivation → Export → Perth GMP → ChemCentre TGO93 Release → Delivered.
Step 5 — SKUs
Present Relevant SKUs — Max 3
Match SKUs to their top patient indications. Lead with indication, then product: "For your chronic pain patients — Maui Wowie 2.0, clinical score 0.71, Beta-Caryophyllene dominant." Show COA for lead SKU.
Step 6 — Handle
Objections
Stay calm. Acknowledge first, then bridge. "I understand — we're not asking you to switch, just to trial one SKU." Refer to objection sheet for specific responses.
Step 7 — Close
Ask for Trial Order or Next Step
Pharmacy: "Would you be comfortable with a trial order — 10 units of 2 SKUs — 30 days, no commitment?" Clinic: "Would you be open to trialling this with 2–3 patients over the next 30 days?" Never leave without a concrete next step and a confirmed date.
Step 8 — Leave
Materials + Confirm Follow-Up
Leave product info sheet, COA summary, business card. Confirm next step out loud: "So I'll follow up on [date] — does that work?" Send calendar confirmation by WhatsApp or email within 2 hours.
Step 9 — Log
CRM Entry — Immediately After Visit
Record: who you met, pain points, SKUs shown, objections raised, outcome (trial agreed / follow-up booked / not interested), next action with date. Never rely on memory.
