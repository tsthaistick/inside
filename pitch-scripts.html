Pitch Scripts

Cold Call · Email · In-Person  ×  Pharmacy · Doctor / Clinic · Wholesale

Ground Rules — Apply to Every Interaction
Rules
One Job Per Interaction Cold call → book a meeting only. Email → get a reply or call only. Meeting → trial order or confirmed next step. Never pitch product on a cold call.
Lead with Their Problem Before showing any SKU, ask what frustrates them about their current supply. Stockouts? Slow delivery? Bad paperwork? Find the pain — then present Thai Stick as the solution.
Speak Their Language Pharmacy: delivery speed, S8 docs, margin. Doctor: indication, terpene profile, COA, patient outcome. Wholesale: supply chain risk, GMP documentation, commercial terms.
One-Sentence Story "Thai Stick cultivates pharmaceutical-grade cannabis flower in Thailand, imports into our GMP-certified facility in Perth, and distributes across Australia."
Cold Calls
1A — Cold Call to Pharmacy
Audience: Pharmacy / Dispensary
Objective

Reach Dispensary Manager or Pharmacist-in-Charge. Book a 20-minute in-person meeting. Target call length: 90 seconds to 3 minutes.

Script
[Front counter answers] "Hi, good morning. My name is [Name], calling from Thai Stick — we're a GMP-certified medicinal cannabis supplier. Could I please speak with your Dispensary Manager or Pharmacist-in-Charge?" [If asked what it's regarding] "It's regarding our medicinal cannabis flower supply range. We're a Perth-based packing and distribution operation. It'll only take two minutes." [When decision-maker answers] "Hi [Name], thank you for taking my call — I'll keep this very brief. My name is [Name] from Thai Stick. We import pharmaceutical-grade cannabis flower from our cultivation facility in Thailand to our own GMP-certified facility here in Perth. I'd love to come in for 20 minutes at a time that suits you. Would sometime this week or next work?"
Key Rules

Never mention price on a cold call. Always end by confirming meeting date/time.

✗ Objection: "We already have a supplier."
That's great — we're not asking you to switch. We're asking for 20 minutes to show you our range. Having a local Perth backup when your current supplier delays is genuinely useful. Would that be worth a quick conversation?
The Close

Perfect — I'll come in to [pharmacy name] on [day] at [time]. I'll bring our product range and COA documentation. Could I grab your email to send a quick calendar confirmation?

1B — Cold Call to Doctor / Clinic
Audience: Doctor / Cannabis Clinic
Objective

Reach Practice Manager or Medical Director. Book a 20-minute clinical briefing or lunch-and-learn session. Never ask to speak to the doctor directly — they are with patients.

Script
[Receptionist answers] "Good morning — my name is [Name] from Thai Stick, we're a medicinal cannabis supplier. Could I please speak with your Practice Manager or Medical Director regarding our clinical product range?" [When Practice Manager answers] "Hi [Name], I'll keep this brief. My name is [Name] from Thai Stick. We cultivate pharmaceutical-grade cannabis flower in Thailand, import to our GMP-certified facility in Perth. We've developed a six-SKU flower range where every product is terpene-profiled and clinically positioned against — chronic pain, anxiety, sleep disorder, PTSD, depression, and ADHD. I'd love to come in for a 20-minute clinical briefing — or if your prescribers do lunch sessions, I'm happy to bring lunch and run a brief clinical walkthrough. Would either of those work?"
Key Rules

Always go through Practice Manager — never the doctor directly. Say 'clinical briefing' not 'sales visit'. Offer lunch-and-learn — standard in Australian medical sales. Never mention price.

✗ Objection: "We don't take meetings with suppliers."
I completely respect that — this isn't a traditional sales pitch. It's a 20-minute clinical walkthrough of how our terpene profiles map to specific patient indications. Would a brief informal lunch session work — no obligation at all?
The Close

Excellent — I'll come in on [day] at [time] for a 20-minute clinical briefing. Should I bring lunch for your team, or would you prefer a straight product meeting? Who should I ask for when I arrive?

In-Person Meetings
3A — In-Person: Pharmacy
Audience: Pharmacy / Dispensary
Objective

Present business case. Handle objections. Secure trial order commitment before leaving the room.

Script
OPENING (2 min): Do not open product folder immediately. Ask first: "Before I show you what we've got — what does your current supply arrangement look like for cannabis flower? Specifically around reliability and lead times?" MIDDLE (10–12 min): Sequence: 1. Acknowledge their pain: "That's consistent with what a lot of WA dispensaries tell us." 2. Thai Stick model (3 sentences): Import from Thailand, Perth GMP, 24hr WA delivery. 3. Show 2–3 SKUs matched to their patient indications. COA for lead SKU. 4. Walk through S8 logistics: chain-of-custody, ScriptCheckWA docs included with every delivery. 5. Pricing: never volunteer it. Wait until they ask. Then: "I'll send a formal pricing sheet after this meeting so you can compare apples to apples." READING THE ROOM: Leaning forward + questions = move to close. Leaning back + short answers = slow down, ask open question: "Is there something specific you'd need to see that I haven't shown you yet?"
Key Rules

Arrive exactly on time. Respect the agreed time. Listen 60% of the time. Take notes. Never use high-pressure tactics. Never give verbal prices in first meeting.

✗ Objection: "Your product is not something our patients have asked for."
That's often because it hasn't been prescribed yet — patient demand follows prescriber behaviour. Would it help if I followed up with the clinics that refer to your pharmacy to introduce our clinical range?
The Close

"Based on what you've shared — I think a trial order of two or three SKUs would be a sensible starting point. 30 days, no long-term commitment. Would you be comfortable moving forward with that?" If yes — take the order on the spot. If not yet — confirm specific reason and hard next step with a date.

3B — In-Person: Doctor / Clinic
Audience: Doctor / Cannabis Clinic
Objective

Build clinical credibility. Match SKUs to patient indications. Get commitment to trial one or two products with 2–3 patients over 30 days.

Script
OPENING: "Thank you for making time — I'll keep to the 20 minutes I asked for. Before I walk through our range, could I ask — across your patient cohort right now, what are the top 2–3 indications where you're finding cannabis most clinically useful or where you're still looking for better options?" CLINICAL BRIDGE (based on their answer): • Chronic pain → MAUI WOWIE 2.0 (score 0.71, Beta-Caryophyllene dominant) • Anxiety → PRIZEFIGHTER (score 0.64, Linalool dominant) • Sleep → TROP CHERRY BX1 (score 0.42, Myrcene dominant — 16.59% THC, ideal for new patients) • PTSD → RUNTZ MINTS (score 0.37) or PRIZEFIGHTER • ADHD → DA FUNK (score 0.30, Alpha-Pinene + Limonene) • Migraine → PRIZEFIGHTER (score 0.66 — strongest migraine SKU) Show COA alongside terpene-symptom matrix. Walk through it methodically.
Key Rules

Australian doctors value evidence and directness. Position yourself as a clinical resource, not a sales rep. Calm, peer-level, evidence-based is the winning tone.

✗ Objection: "Can you make therapeutic claims about this product?"
In the strict regulatory sense, no — ARTG registration is required for formal therapeutic claims and no cannabis flower has that. We present terpene research data and COA. The prescribing decision is entirely yours based on your clinical judgment.
The Close

"Based on what you've told me — would you be comfortable trialling Maui Wowie 2.0 with two or three of your chronic pain patients over 30 days? I'll make sure your preferred pharmacy has stock ready, and I'll follow up in four weeks to hear how it went clinically. No obligation beyond that."
