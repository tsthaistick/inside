Objection Response Guide

Every objection you will hear in the field — with a direct, confident response. Review before any meeting.

Objection Your Response
"We already have a supplier." "We're not asking you to switch — just to add a locally packed backup. One call when your current supplier delays."
"We're not taking new suppliers." "I'm only asking for 20 minutes, not a commitment. After that, the decision is entirely yours."
"Send me some information." "Happy to — before I do, what are your top two patient indications? I'll make sure what I send is actually relevant."
"Your product is not TGA registered." "Correct — no cannabis flower is ARTG registered in Australia. All products including your current supplier operate under SAS-B and AP prescribing pathways. Our TGO93 compliance is equivalent to any other supplier on the market."
"We don't know your brand." "That's exactly why I'm here. I'm not asking for blind trust — I'm asking for a 30-day trial. The COA and GMP documentation will tell you everything about our quality."
"Your product is grown in Thailand — is that an issue?" "Not under TGA import regulations. The TGA evaluates imported cannabis on GMP equivalence and TGO93 compliance — not country of origin. Our import licence and COA documentation confirm we meet every requirement."
"What if you run out of stock?" "We hold buffer stock at our Perth packing facility specifically to prevent that. Because we pack in Perth, we have physical stock on the ground in WA ready for same-day dispatch — unlike interstate suppliers who depend entirely on their supply chain."
"Your pricing is too high." "Let me send you a detailed comparison that factors in WA freight costs from the Eastern States. Once you account for that, our landed cost is very competitive. Can I follow up with that breakdown today?"
"We need head office approval." "Of course. What documentation does your head office need to evaluate a new supplier? I'll prepare a specific pack so the review is as straightforward as possible for them."
"We're too busy right now." "Completely understand. When would be a better time — would [specific date two weeks out] work to revisit this?"
