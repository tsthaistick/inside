A. Core Regulatory Terms — Know Every One Without Hesitation
TermFull NameWhat It Controls / DoesWhy It Matters for Thai Stick
TGA Therapeutic Goods Administration Australia's medicines regulator — equivalent to the FDA. Controls what can be imported, prescribed, manufactured, and sold. Sets all standards for medicinal cannabis. Every Thai Stick product entering Australia must meet TGA standards. Without TGA approval pathways, we cannot legally supply a single gram.
TGO 93 Therapeutic Goods Order 93 The TGA's specific quality standard for medicinal cannabis. Sets strict limits on: THC/CBD potency accuracy, pesticide residues, microbial contamination, heavy metals, moisture content, foreign matter. Every batch imported from Thailand must pass TGO93 testing (via ChemCentre in Perth) before release. Our COA is the proof. If a batch fails — it cannot enter the supply chain.
ODC Office of Drug Control Government body issuing import and export licences for cannabis. Two requirements: (1) ODC Import Licence — company-level approval, and (2) ODC Import Permit — issued per shipment. Thai Stick must hold an active ODC Import Licence. Every individual shipment from Thailand requires a separate ODC Import Permit before it departs. This is our #1 pre-commercial step.
SAS-B Special Access Scheme Category B The most common prescription pathway. Any registered doctor can apply to TGA to prescribe for an individual patient — no specialist qualification required. This is how most of Australia's ~1 million annual cannabis prescriptions are written. Getting Thai Stick onto the SAS-B approved product list means any doctor can find and prescribe us.
AP Authorised Prescriber A doctor who applies once to TGA to prescribe for an entire category of patients — no individual TGA approval needed per script. Much higher prescribing volume than SAS-B. Cannabis clinics (Alternaleaf, PlantMed, Candor) operate mainly on AP pathways. One AP clinic = hundreds of monthly scripts. Getting AP clinics to adopt Thai Stick = high volume, low friction.
S8 Schedule 8 Controlled Drug Drug classification for all THC-containing cannabis products. Requires: real-time prescription monitoring (ScriptCheckWA/SafeScript), closed-loop S8 transport, vault storage, chain-of-custody on every delivery. All 6 Thai Stick flower SKUs are Schedule 8. Our Perth facility handles all S8 dispatch documentation. Every delivery must have a signed chain-of-custody form.
S4 Schedule 4 Prescription Medicine Classification for CBD-only products under 1% THC. Less restricted than S8 — no prescription monitoring system required. Thai Stick does not currently have S4 products. All our flower contains meaningful THC content. Worth noting for future CBD product expansion.
COA Certificate of Analysis The batch test report proving a product meets TGO93 standards. Issued by an accredited lab. Covers: potency, terpenes, microbials, pesticides, heavy metals, moisture. Thai Stick provides two COAs per batch: Thai lab COA (pre-export) + ChemCentre COA (TGO93 release, Australia). Both must be available to buyers on request.
GMP Good Manufacturing Practice International quality standard for pharmaceutical manufacturing. Covers: facility cleanliness, staff training, batch records, equipment calibration, and full product traceability. Our Perth packing facility is GMP-certified. Our Thai cultivation facility operates to GMP-equivalent standards required for TGA import approval.
GACP Good Agricultural and Collection Practices WHO standard for quality control from seed to harvest. Covers cultivation inputs, harvesting, post-harvest handling, and documentation. Thai Stick's Thai facility is GACP certified — the required standard for cannabis cultivation destined for pharmaceutical use. Verified by independent audit.
ScriptCheckWA Prescription Monitoring System — WA WA's real-time prescription monitoring database. All S8 prescriptions must be checked and recorded. Mandatory for all WA prescribers since June 2025. WA pharmacies and prescribers use ScriptCheckWA for every Thai Stick S8 transaction. Our S8 delivery documentation is pre-formatted for ScriptCheckWA compatibility.
SafeScript Prescription Monitoring System — VIC Victoria's equivalent of ScriptCheckWA. Mandatory check before prescribing or dispensing S8 medicines in VIC. All VIC pharmacy and clinic accounts must use SafeScript. Our documentation is compliant.
AHPRA Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency Registers all practising doctors, nurses, and pharmacists in Australia. Every prescriber has an AHPRA registration number. We collect AHPRA numbers from all clinic accounts. Verify doctor registration at ahpra.gov.au before first supply.
ARTG Australian Register of Therapeutic Goods The TGA's official register of approved therapeutic goods. ARTG registration = formal product approval to make therapeutic claims. No cannabis flower product in Australia has ARTG registration. All products (including competitors) operate under SAS-B/AP pathways. We cannot make therapeutic claims — only present clinical terpene data.
FEFO First Expiry, First Out Standard pharmaceutical stock rotation practice — products with the earliest expiry date are dispatched first. Thai Stick's Perth facility operates strict FEFO. Ensures patients receive the freshest possible product and no expired stock reaches the supply chain.
